The day
I hit thirty-nine, I
allowed him to stroke
my globe of a cheek. His flesh,
my flesh flowed. He said, Open wide, poured
olive oil down my throat. Soon you’ll be forty…
he whispered, and how could I not roll over on
top. I rolled and he drowned in my flesh. I
drowned his dying sentence out. I left him
there for six hours that felt like a week.
His mouth slightly open, his eyes
bulging with greed.

There was nothing else left in the house to eat.
